<p><ul><li>Composite Brake Shoes</li><li>Powder Metallurgy Brake Shoes</li><li>Cast Iron Brake Shoes</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The railway brake shoes market consists of three types: composite brake shoes, powder metallurgy brake shoes, and cast iron brake shoes. Composite brake shoes are made from a combination of materials such as phenolic resins and fiberglass, offering excellent heat resistance and low wear rates. Powder metallurgy brake shoes are manufactured using powdered metals that are pressed and sintered together, providing superior strength, durability, and consistent friction characteristics. Cast iron brake shoes, on the other hand, are produced by pouring molten cast iron into molds, which are cost-effective and widely used in various railway applications.</p></p>
