<p><p>Pectin is a type of soluble fiber found in fruits that is widely used as a gelling agent in various food and beverage products. There are three main types of pectin in the market: High Methoxyl (HM) Pectin, Low Methoxyl (LMC) Pectin, and Amidated low Methoxyl (LMA) Pectin. HM pectin is used in high-sugar products like jams and jellies, LMC pectin is used in low-sugar or no-sugar products, and LMA pectin is used in dairy and protein-based products to provide stability and improve texture.</p></p>
