<p><p>Coated paper is classified into two main types: coated chemical pulp and coated mechanical pulp. Coated chemical pulp refers to paper produced using chemically  treated pulp fibers, which results in a higher quality product. It is generally used for premium printing applications such as magazines, catalogs, and brochures. On the other hand, coated mechanical pulp is made from mechanically ground wood fibers, making it less expensive but also of lower quality. It is commonly used for newspapers, inserts, and other less demanding print materials.</p></p>
