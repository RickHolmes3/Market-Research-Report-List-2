<p><p>The papermaking retention agent market consists of different types of chemicals used to improve the retention of fibers and fillers in the papermaking process. Polyacrylamide (PAM) is a widely used retention agent due to its high effectiveness in flocculating fine particles. Polyethyleneimine (PEI) is a cationic polymer that enhances the retention of anionic materials in papermaking. Colloidal silica is a suspension of fine particles that acts as a retention aid by providing a larger surface area for bonding with fibers. Bentonite is a clay mineral that improves the retention of fillers and helps in drainage during paper formation.</p></p>
