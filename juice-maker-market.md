<p><p>Juice makers are available in various types - centrifugal, masticating, and triturating. Centrifugal juice makers use high-speed spinning blades to extract juice quickly, but they may generate heat and oxidize the nutrients. Masticating juice makers use a slow crushing and squeezing action, preserving more nutrients and producing a pulpier juice. Triturating juice makers are similar to masticating ones but with two gears that rotate slowly, resulting in highly nutritious, smooth, and foam-free juices. Each type offers different benefits, making the choice dependent on personal preferences and desired juice quality.</p></p>
