<p><p>Solid lasers are classified into two main types: pulse type and continuous type. Pulse type lasers emit high-energy laser beams for a short period, followed by a period of rest. They are used in applications that require a high peak power, such as materials processing and scientific research. Continuous type lasers, on the other hand, emit a continuous laser beam with constant power output. They are commonly used for cutting, welding, and medical procedures. Both types have their unique applications and are important in various industries.</p></p>
