Hemicellulose plays a crucial role in maintaining the structure and integrity of plant cell walls, as well as in regulating water absorption and nutrient transportation.</p><p>In recent years, there has been a growing demand for plant hemicellulose due to its various applications across several industries. One of its major applications is in the production of biofuels and biochemicals, where it can be converted into ethanol, xylitol, furfural, and other value-added products. Plant hemicellulose is also used as a thickening and gelling agent in the food industry, as well as in the production of paper, textiles, and pharmaceuticals.</p><p>The plant hemicellulose market is witnessing significant growth and is expected to continue to expand in the coming years.
