<p><p>The polyurethane acrylate market can be divided into three main types: oligomers, monomers, and others. Oligomers are large molecules formed by combining smaller molecules, known as monomers. These oligomers provide the structural backbone of polyurethane acrylate products. Monomers, on the other hand, are smaller molecules that can combine to form oligomers or act as reactive agents during the curing process. The "others" category encompasses various additives, fillers, and stabilizers used in the production of polyurethane acrylate.</p></p>
