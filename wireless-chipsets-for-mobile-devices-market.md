<p><p>Wireless chipsets for mobile devices are classified into two market types: separate chips and integrated chips. Separate chips refer to individual components that are designed and sold separately, such as the Wi-Fi chip, Bluetooth chip, and cellular chip. These chips are developed by different manufacturers and need to be assembled into a single circuit board. In contrast, integrated chips combine multiple functionalities into a single chip, offering a more compact and efficient solution. Integrated chips are typically developed by a single manufacturer, making the manufacturing process simpler and reducing costs.</p></p>
