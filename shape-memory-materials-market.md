<p><p>Shape memory materials are unique materials that can remember their original shape and regain it after being deformed. There are three types in the market:</p><p>1. Alloy materials: These shape memory materials are often metallic alloys, such as nickel-titanium (NiTi) or copper-based materials. They exhibit excellent shape memory properties and are widely used in various applications, including automotive, aerospace, and medical devices.</p><p>2. Polymer materials: These shape memory materials are composed of polymers, such as shape memory polymers (SMPs). SMPs can change their shape in response to external stimuli, such as temperature or light. They find applications in fields like soft robotics, textiles, and biomedicine.</p><p>3. Others: This category includes shape memory materials that do not fall into the alloy or polymer category. It may involve materials like ceramics or composites that exhibit shape memory properties and are used in specific niche applications.</p></p>
