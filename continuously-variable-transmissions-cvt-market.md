<p><p>A Continuously Variable Transmission (CVT) is an automatic transmission system that provides seamless gear ratio changes without the use of traditional gears. Instead of fixed gears, CVTs use a belt or a chain and pulley system that allows for an infinite number of gear ratios. This allows the engine to operate at its most efficient RPM (revolutions per minute) range and provides a smoother driving experience.</p><p>The CVT market has been witnessing significant growth over the past few years and is expected to continue this trend in the coming years. <p><p>Continuously Variable Transmissions (CVT) are mechanisms that provide a smooth and seamless transition between gear ratios without discrete steps. The CVT market can be divided into two types: Belt-Chain Drive CVTs and Planetary CVTs. Belt-Chain Drive CVTs use a system of pulleys and a belt or chain to transmit power, providing a wide range of gear ratios. On the other hand, Planetary CVTs employ a complex system of gears and multiple planetary gear sets to achieve variable ratios. Both types cater to the demand for efficient and smooth power transmission in various industries.</p></p>
