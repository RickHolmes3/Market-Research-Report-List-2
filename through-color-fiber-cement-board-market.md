<p><p>The through-color fiber cement board market consists of three major types: thin board, conventional board, and thick board. Thin board refers to fiber cement boards that have a smaller thickness, typically used for interior applications such as ceilings and walls. Conventional board refers to fiber cement boards with a moderate thickness, commonly used for both interior and exterior applications like cladding and siding. Thick board, on the other hand, refers to fiber cement boards with a larger thickness, typically utilized for heavy-duty exterior applications such as exterior walls in high-rise buildings or industrial structures.</p></p>
