<p><p>The superabsorbent polymers (SAP) market consists of three main types: starch-based SAP, cellulose-based SAP, and acrylic resin SAP. Starch-based SAP is made by modifying starch molecules to increase their water-absorbing capacity. Cellulose-based SAP is derived from cellulose, a natural polymer found in plants, and offers excellent absorption and biodegradability. Acrylic resin SAP is produced by polymerizing acrylic acid, resulting in a highly absorbent material with a wide range of applications. These SAP types contribute to the diverse market for superabsorbent polymers, which are widely used in personal care products, agriculture, and medical applications.</p></p>
