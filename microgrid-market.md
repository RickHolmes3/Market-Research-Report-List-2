<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Microgrid market types can be categorized as grid-tied and independent. In a grid-tied microgrid, it is connected to the main power grid and can import electricity when its own generation falls short or export excess electricity back to the grid. This market type allows for increased resilience and reliability during emergencies or when there are grid disruptions. On the other hand, an independent microgrid operates in isolation from the main power grid, often utilizing renewable energy sources for its generation. It provides self-sufficiency and can be beneficial for remote areas or critical facilities.</p></p>
