<p><strong>The Radiation-Hardened Electronics Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Radiation Hardening by Design (RHBD)</li><li>Radiation Hardening by Process (RHBP)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Radiation-hardened electronics are designed specifically to operate reliably in environments with high levels of radiation, such as nuclear power plants or space missions. There are two main types of radiation-hardening techniques: Radiation Hardening by Design (RHBD) and Radiation Hardening by Process (RHBP). </p><p>RHBD involves modifying the electronic components and system architecture to withstand radiation effects, including shielding, redundancy, and specific material selection. On the other hand, RHBP refers to using specific manufacturing processes and materials to make the electronic devices more resilient to radiation, such as designing transistors with thicker oxide layers or using radiation-tolerant materials. Both approaches are crucial for ensuring the integrity and longevity of electronics in radiation-intensive environments.</p></p>
