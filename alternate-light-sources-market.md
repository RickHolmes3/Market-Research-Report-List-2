<p><p>Alternate light sources (ALS) are devices that emit various wavelengths of light, typically outside the visible spectrum, for the purpose of detecting and analyzing a range of materials. Factors contributing to market growth include the increasing demand for alternative light sources in forensics and law enforcement agencies, the rising use of ALS in industrial inspections for quality control, and the growing need for counterfeit detection in various sectors such as banking, automotive, and consumer goods.</p><p>The market is also witnessing several trends that are shaping its growth. One notable trend is the development of portable and handheld ALS devices, which offer greater convenience and ease of use compared to traditional bulky and fixed systems. This trend is particularly prominent in the field of forensics, enabling investigators to easily transport and use ALS at crime scenes.</p><p>Another trend is the integration of advanced technologies such as UV-C LEDs (Ultraviolet-C Light Emitting Diodes), which provide better performance, lower energy consumption, and longer lifespan compared to conventional light sources.
