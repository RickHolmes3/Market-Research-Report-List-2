<p><p>A torsional damper, also known as a harmonic balancer or a vibration damper, is a device used to reduce torsional vibrations and oscillations in mechanical systems. It consists of a free rotating inertia ring surrounded by a rubber material, which absorbs and dampens the vibrations caused by the engine or rotating machinery.</p><p>The torsional damper market is expected to experience significant growth in the coming years. The increasing demand for efficient and high-performance vehicles, coupled with stringent government regulations for emissions and fuel efficiency, is driving the market growth. Torsional dampers help in reducing engine vibrations, enhancing fuel efficiency, and improving overall performance of the vehicle. These advantages are attracting automotive manufacturers to adopt torsional dampers in their vehicles.</p><p>Furthermore, the growing adoption of hybrid and electric vehicles is expected to further boost the demand for torsional dampers. These vehicles have a different powertrain system compared to traditional internal combustion engines, which results in different types of vibrations. Torsional dampers play a crucial role in reducing vibrations and ensuring smooth operation of the powertrain system in hybrid and electric vehicles.</p><p>In addition, the increasing awareness about the harmful effects of vibrations on machinery and equipment is driving the demand for torsional dampers in industrial applications. Vibrations can lead to equipment failure, increased maintenance costs, and decreased productivity. Torsional dampers help in minimizing these vibrations, leading to improved operational efficiency and reduced downtime.</p><p>Overall, with the increasing demand for vehicles with better performance and reduced vibrations, and the growing adoption of hybrid and electric vehicles, the torsional damper market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 13.4% during the forecasted period.
