<p><p>The proximity sensor is an essential feature in smartphones that detects the presence of nearby objects without physical contact. There are two main types of proximity sensors available in the market: optical displacement sensors and ultrasonic displacement sensors. Optical displacement sensors utilize infrared light to measure the distance between the sensor and an object, while ultrasonic displacement sensors emit ultrasonic waves and calculate the time it takes for the waves to bounce back to determine proximity. Both types are widely used in smartphones to enable functions such as auto screen off during phone calls or gesture-based controls.</p></p>
