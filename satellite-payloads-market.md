<p><p>Satellite payloads refer to the instruments or equipment installed on a satellite to perform specific tasks. LEO (Low Earth orbit) payloads are typically used for remote sensing, Earth observation, and scientific research. GEO (Geosynchronous Earth orbit) payloads are mainly utilized for communication and broadcasting services due to their fixed position above the Earth. MEO (Medium Earth orbit) payloads offer a compromise between LEO and GEO, providing improved communication and navigation capabilities. Each market type caters to different applications and offers unique advantages based on their orbital characteristics.</p></p>
