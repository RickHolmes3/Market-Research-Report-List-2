<p><p>Hybrid FPGA refers to combinations of Field Programmable Gate Array (FPGA) with other technologies to enhance performance and functionality. FPGA-CPU denotes integrating FPGA with a central processing unit, enabling customized hardware acceleration alongside traditional CPU tasks. FPGA-Memory involves pairing FPGA with high-speed memory, enabling efficient data processing and storage. FPGA-MCU implies combining FPGA with a microcontroller unit, offering embedded processing capabilities and reconfigurable hardware. FPGA-Converter represents the coupling of FPGA with a converter, facilitating real-time data transformation. These market types highlight the diverse applications and benefits of Hybrid FPGA in various industries such as telecommunications, automotive, and aerospace.</p></p>
