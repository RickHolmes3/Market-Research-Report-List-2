<p><p>The Pulp & Paper Enzymes market offers several types of enzymes that play a crucial role in the papermaking industry. Amylases are used to break down starch, while Xylanases/Hemicellulases degrade xylan and hemicellulose. Lipases aid in removing fats and waxes, while Cellulose enzymes break down cellulose fibers. Pectinases assist in removing pectin, a component of plant cell walls. These enzymes enhance the efficiency of various papermaking processes, including deinking, refining, and bleaching. Overall, the diverse range of enzymes in this market helps optimize paper production and improve its quality.</p></p>
