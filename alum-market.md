<p><p>Alum, also known as potassium alum or aluminum potassium sulfate, is a chemical compound widely used in various industries. It is an inorganic salt that finds applications as a deodorant, water purifier, flame retardant, mordant in textile dyeing, and antiperspirant, among others. Alum is commonly found naturally in minerals like alunite and is commercially produced through chemical processes, making it readily available for various industrial uses.</p><p>The alum market has witnessed significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue its upward trajectory during the forecast period. The market growth can be attributed to factors such as the increasing demand for alum in the water treatment industry, growing textile industry, rising need for flame retardant materials, and expanding population leading to rising hygiene concerns.</p><p>In the water treatment industry, alum is extensively used as a primary coagulant due to its excellent flocculation and sedimentation properties. With the rising demand for clean and safe drinking water worldwide, the demand for alum in the water treatment sector is expected to surge. Additionally, the growing textile industry, particularly in emerging economies, is driving the demand for alum as a mordant in dyeing processes.</p><p>Furthermore, the increasing emphasis on fire safety regulations in various industries has led to a rise in the application of flame retardant materials, where alum finds wide usage.
