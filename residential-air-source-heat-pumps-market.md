<p><p>Residential Air Source Heat Pumps (ASHPs) are heating and cooling systems that transfer heat between the indoor and outdoor environments using electricity and refrigerant. ASHPs are an energy-efficient alternative to traditional fossil fuel-based heating and cooling systems, as they can provide up to 4 units of heat for every unit of electricity used.</p><p>The future outlook for the Residential ASHPs market is promising due to the growing demand for energy-efficient heating and cooling solutions. <p><p>Residential air source heat pumps come in two main types: air-to-air and air-to-water. Air-to-air heat pumps transfer heat from the outside air to the inside of the building using a fan and a duct system. These are commonly used for heating and cooling the indoor air. On the other hand, air-to-water heat pumps transfer heat from the outside air to a water-based system, such as radiators or underfloor heating, providing both space heating and hot water. These heat pumps are suitable for buildings that require both heating and domestic hot water.</p></p>
