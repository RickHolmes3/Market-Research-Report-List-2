<p><p>Cellulose Nanofibers (CNF) are microscopic, rod-like fibers derived from natural cellulose materials, such as wood and plants. The increased focus on renewable and recyclable materials across industries, such as packaging, textiles, automotive, and construction, is driving the demand for CNF. CNF is used as a reinforcing agent in composites, enhancing the mechanical properties of materials while reducing their weight. It is also used in the production of films, coatings, and membranes for applications in flexible electronics, sensors, and energy storage devices.</p><p>The market is also supported by extensive research and development activities aimed at improving the production process and expanding the application areas of CNF.
