<p><p>UV stabilizers are additives used in plastics to protect them from the harmful effects of ultraviolet (UV) radiation. There are different types of UV stabilizers available in the market. Hindered amine light stabilizers (HALS) work by scavenging free radicals and prolonging the lifespan of the plastic. UV absorbers absorb UV radiation and convert it into heat energy, preventing damage to the plastic. Quenchers prevent the formation of reactive species by dispersing the absorbed UV energy. Other types of UV stabilizers include light stabilizers and photostabilizers, which protect against the degradation caused by UV radiation.</p></p>
