<p><p>Flip chip packaging is a technique used in the semiconductor industry to mount microchips onto carrier substrates for electronic devices. It involves electrically interconnecting the chip with the substrate using metal bumps (solder balls or copper pillars) instead of traditional wire bonding. This packaging method offers numerous benefits, including higher I/O density, improved electrical performance, and better thermal dissipation.</p><p>The flip chip packages market is witnessing significant growth, driven by the increasing demand for compact electronic devices with enhanced performance and reliability. The rise in the adoption of advanced packaging technologies in the automotive, consumer electronics, and healthcare sectors is further fueling market growth.</p><p>Moreover, the growing popularity of wearable devices, such as smartwatches and fitness trackers, is contributing to the market expansion.
